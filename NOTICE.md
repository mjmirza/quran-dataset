# Notice and attribution

This dataset is free to use under CC BY 4.0. It stands on public domain Quran text plus a small amount of openly licensed structural metadata. This file records exactly what came from where, so anyone can reuse it with confidence and keep the credit correct.

## The Arabic Quran text

The text of the Quran is public domain. The specific digital edition used here is the Uthmani script from the Tanzil Project, reviewed by Hafiz Husain Al Awjy and distributed through the King Fahd Glorious Quran Printing Complex. The Tanzil edition is provided under Creative Commons Attribution 3.0, so this credit travels with the text.

## The structural metadata

The surah names, revelation type and order, and the boundaries for juz, hizb, manzil, ruku, mushaf page, and sajda come from the Tanzil metadata file. It is provided under Creative Commons Attribution 3.0.

- Asset. Quran structural metadata
- Origin. Tanzil quran-data.xml
- License. CC BY 3.0
- Source. https://tanzil.net/res/text/metadata/quran-data.xml

## The canonical structure

The counts of 114 surahs and 6236 ayahs, and the standard division of the mushaf, follow the King Fahd Glorious Quran Printing Complex. This is used as an authority to validate against, not copied content.

## The independent cross check

One validation layer compares the bare consonantal letters of every surah against a second, separately published Quran dataset, purely as a second opinion. None of that dataset is included or redistributed here. Only the pass or fail result of the comparison is recorded.
